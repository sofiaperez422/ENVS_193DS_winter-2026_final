**Broad research question(s) and analysis**

*1. How does visual obstruction at the nest and distance to Prairie dog colony edge affect probability of Mountain Plover nest site use?*

As visual obstruction at the nest increases, the probability of Mountain Plover nest site use decreases. Similarly, as distance to Prairie dog colony edge increases, the probability of Mountain Plover nest site use decreases. Both of these relationships can be explained by the fact that soil disturbance activity and vegetation clipping done by prairie dogs make for an ideal nesting habitat for Mountain Plovers due to the coverage from little visual vegetation obstruction that also allows them to detect approaching ground predators.

*2. Is there a simpler model that explains probability of Mountain Plover nest site use, and if so, what is it?*

A generalized linear model can explain the probability of Mountain Plover nest site use by visually showing model predictions of the probability curve of nest site use as the predictor variable, in this case visual obstruction, increases.
